Conquest:
A Game of War

Terms

Soldier: Cards numbered two through ten.

Commander: Face cards, aces and jokers.

Field: The play area.

Spoils: Tricks taken and kept by the winning player each hand.

Cards

Soldier Cards:

In one round of play, the highest card value wins, or in the case of a tie, the highest suit wins. In descending order, the suits are Spades, Diamonds, Clubs, Hearts.

Commander Cards:

Commanders are played after all soldiers have been revealed, and are played in clockwise order starting with the player to the left of the dealer. At the beginning of each round, each player that does not have a commander must draw one from the Commander pile.

Jack:
You may exchange your Soldier on the field with a Soldier from your hand.

Queen:
You may force another player to trade Soldiers with you.

King:
You may discard any number of your soldiers, including the one on the field, and draw that many soldiers from the deck. If you discard your field soldier, you must play a new field soldier from your hand.

Ace:
Aces are played on the field on top of your soldier, and automatically beat all Soldiers, and in the event of multiple Aces, the highest suit wins. Aces on the field are not traded by the Queen and remain in play under the initial player.

Joker:
The Joker can be played two ways:
Option 1: All commanders on the field are nullified, and a new hand is played. New commanders are not drawn for this hand.
Option 2: One commander is stopped. A new hand is not played.

Order of Play

The Buy

In order to play, players must place their Buy into the Pot. The Buy may be different depending on who or where the game is played. High stakes Conquest may happen in castles among nobles, or between proud and affluent ship captains in some of the larger ports. Low stakes Conquest is more common, and can be found in most taverns or ports, and often friends. Additionally, the Buy may consist of things other than Coin, such as a desired good, a promised service, or some other non-monetary trade, but the players must agree on the value of the Buy.

The Deal

Each player is dealt a number of soldiers equal to the number of players +1.

The Round

1. Each player who doesn't have a Commander draws one Commander.

2. Each player plays a Soldier, face down, from their hand onto the field.

3. Once all players have a Soldier on the field, all Soldiers are revealed.

4. The highest value Soldier on the field wins the hand, ties are determined by suit.

5. Commanders may now be played in turn order, clockwise from the dealer.

6. After all Commanders are resolved and the hand won, the next round begins.
