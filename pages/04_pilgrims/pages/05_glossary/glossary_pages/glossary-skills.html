Skills

Skills are divided into two categories: "General" and "Combat." These names should give the player an idea of the intended purpose of the skill; however, players should always be encouraged to play creatively; if a General skill seems useful during combat or a Combat skill could be used outside of a fight, then talk about it with the GM.

Cost
Displays the number of skill points needed to acquire the skill. Skill points are spent during downtime.

Requirement
Shows a skill's requirement. For almost all skills, a certain threshold for a Characteristic or Capability must be met prior to aqcuiring the skill in question.

General Skills

Herbalism

Requirement: No Capability Requirement
Cost: 2 Skill Points

Given you have herbalist's supplies, you are able to spend thirty minutes creating a potion. The effects of the potion are determined by the ingredients used.

Multilingual

Requirement: Intellect Score ≥ 10
Cost: 3 Skill Points

Choose a language your character does not currently understand. They are now able to speak this language. If they are able to read and write, you may spend one additional skill point to be able to read and write in this language.

Additionally, any time you encounter a new language group, you may spend one skill point to add that language to the list of languages your character speaks, and one additional skill point to gain reading and writing comprehension in that language.

A list of languages can be found here.

Obfuscation

Requirement: Dexterity Score ≥ 12, Insight OR Intellect Score ≥ 10
Cost: 4 Skill Points

Out of combat, you are able to hide yourself in plain sight. Any character attempting to perceive you must succeed at a Opposed Check of their Psyche against your choice of Dexterity, Insight or Intellect. If you are in view of a character when you utilize this skill, they have advantage on the Check.

In combat, you may choose not to take an action during combat. If you do so, you are not included in that round's Damage Roll.

Psychic Font

Requirement: Psyche Score ≥ 14
Cost: 3 Skill Points

Through clarity of mind you tap into a reserve of energy and are able to restore 1d6 Psyche.

You may use this skill a number of times per day equal to your Psyche modifier.

Reading

Requirement: Intellect Score ≥ 8
Cost: 2 Skill Points

When observing written text in a language you speak, you understand its meaning.

Runescript

Requirement: Intellect Score ≥ 16
Cost: 4 Skill Points

You are able to comprehend runes and pictographic languages, often used in older magical and religious tomes and scrolls.

Twin Mind

Requirement: Psyche Score ≥ 18
Cost: 4 Skill Points

You have trained yourself to offload a portion of mental strain to a willing ally, allowing them to focus for you.

As long as you have a conscious willing ally with available Focus, you may Focus on an additional spell or miracle through them. Any spells cast this way cost 1 additional Psyche.

Writing

Requirement: Intellect Score ≥ 8
Cost: 2 Skill Points

Given that you have the necessary materials, you are able to write messages in a language you speak.

Combat Skills

Advanced Weaponry

Requirement: Intellect Score ≥ 10
Cost: 4 Skill Points

Add your proficiency bonus when attacking with Advanced Weapons.

Colossal Strength

Requirement: Strength Score ≥ 20
Cost: 4 Skill Points

You can wield two-handed weapons with one hand, and can wield two such weapons this way.

Extra Action

Requirement: Dexterity Score ≥ 20
Cost: 4 Skill Points

When in combat you may take an additional action on the party's turn.

Extra Swing

Requirement: Dexterity Score ≥ 12
Cost: 3 Skill Points

When you use your action to attack with a weapon of Small size, you may make one additional attack with that weapon without expending additional Stamina.

Fast Hands

Requirement: Dexterity Score ≥ 10
Cost: 2 Skill Points

Given that you have a pack or bag, you are able to retrieve one small item from it during your turn in combat without spending Stamina.

Great Strength

Requirement: Strength Score ≥ 12
Cost: 3 Skill Points

When rolling damage with weapons, roll one additional d6.

Heavy Armor

Requirement: Stamina Score ≥ 12
Cost: 3 Skill Points

You are capable of wearing Heavy Armor without a penalty to Stamina.

Medium Armor

Requirement: Stamina Score ≥ 10
Cost: 3 Skill Points

You are capable of wearing Medium Armor without a penalty to Stamina.

Psychic Absorption

Requirement: Psyche Score ≥ 12
Cost: 3 Skill Points

Once per round, when you are targeted with a non-physical effect you may roll Xd3, where X is the number of dice rolled for the triggering effect, and add the result to your current Psyche, up to your maximum Psyche.

You may use this skill a number of times equal to your Psyche modifier.

Psychic Surge

Requirement: Psyche Score ≥ 20
Cost: 4 Skill Points

When rolling d6 for non-physical damage or effects, your dice become exploding dice: when you roll a 6, you can roll that die again and add the new result to your total.

Ranged Weaponry

Requirement: Dexterity Score ≥ 10
Cost: 2 Skill Points

Add your proficiency bonus when attacking with Ranged Weapons.

Simple Weaponry

Requirement: Strength OR Dexterity Score ≥ 8
Cost: 1 Skill Points

Add your proficiency bonus when attacking with Simple Weapons.

Class Skills

Beyond the Veil

Requirement: Recluse

While ethereal, you can make melee attacks, but all damage you deal counts as nonphysical. Any Curse accrued while ethereal is doubled.

Bloodlust

Requirement: Knight Errant

When you kill an enemy with a weapon strike in combat, gain one token of blood, up to three. You can then spend tokens of blood while attacking to roll one additional damage die for each token of blood spent.

Dauntless

Requirement: Knight Errant OR Warrior

When you are reduced to zero health, roll 1d6. You are not downed, and instead regain an amount of health equal to the result of the d6 multiplied by your Vitality modifier, up to your max health. Once you utilize this Skill, you cannot do so again until you rest.

Ethereal Veil

Requirement: Recluse OR Sage

You are able to temporarily take on a nonphysical existence. While ethereal, your physical body becomes immaterial and hardly perceptible. As long as you are not moving, you are invisible to anything which relies on regular sight, and you cannot interact with the physical world. For the duration of this effect, your Psyche acts as your health. If your Psyche is reduced to zero, you become material again.

Primeval Call

Requirement: Apostate

You are able to spend Psyche to summon ancient spirits. The Soul Value of the summoned spirit is equal to the amount of Psyche spent to summon it. Once the spirit is summoned, roll 1d6.
• If the result is a one or two, the spirit is hostile towards all living things, and will act at the discretion of the GM.
• If the result is a three, four or five, the spirit is hostile towards all living things, but will attack the enemies of the summoner before attacking their allies.
• If the result is a six, the spirit acts at the discretion of the summoner, but at the end of combat the roll must be made again.

Spell-Craft

Requirement: Warlock OR Mage

When casting a spell, instead of choosing from a list, you are able to simply describe what you would like to happen and how much Psyche you spend, and within the reason of the GM, you will cast that spell.

Summoning

Requirement: Apostate or Monk

You are able to spend Psyche to summon spirits from one of the stories you have recorded. Choose a spirit recorded in your tome, and pay the requisite Psyche to summon them. You may only have one summoned spirit at a time. The spirit you have summoned acts on your turn, is controlled by you, and has its own Stats.

Welling Deep

Requirement: Warlock

While in combat, if you have zero Psyche, you may roll 1d6. The result of this roll becomes temporary Psyche which is only available on the turn you gained it. You may then cast a spell using this Psyche, but any Curse you would accrue is doubled.

Skill Checks

Players may make Skill Checks during downtime, provided they have the requisite materials. For example, if a player wishes for their character to learn another language, they must have some material in that language, or someone to teach it to them.

Skill Checks are modified progressively in three stages: Difficult, Very Hard, and Formidable. Players may only make one Skill Check per day, and once a player succeeds at all three stages, they acquire the corresponding skill without needing to spend a Skill Point.
