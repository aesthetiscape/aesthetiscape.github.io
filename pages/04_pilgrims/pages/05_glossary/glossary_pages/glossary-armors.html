Armors

Quality: Low / High / Special
Quality affects the amount by which incoming damage may be reduced each round of combat.

Weight: Light / Medium / Heavy
Weight affects the Strength requirement to effectively wear Medium and Heavy Armors. If a character's Strength modifier is below the required value, then all Stamina costs during combat are doubled.

Location: Head / Arms / Chest / Legs
Armor can be equipped to one of these four locations. If a body part that would receive a wound has armor equipped, then the armor can be used to mitigate the wound. The number of wounds an armor piece can mitigate is based on its weight.

Quality

Low

Low Quality Armor can reduce incoming damage by 1d3.

High

High Quality Armor can reduce incoming damage by 2d3.

Special

Special Quality Armor can reduce incoming damage by 3d3.

Weight

Light

Light Weight Armor can only mitigate 1 wound before being destroyed, but it has no Strength requirement to wear effectively.

Medium

Medium Weight Armor can mitigate 2 wounds before being destroyed, but if the wearer has a Strength modifier below 1, all Stamina costs during combat are doubled.

Heavy

Heavy Weight Armor can mitigate 3 wounds before being destroyed, but if the wearer has a Strength modifier below 3, all Stamina costs during combat are doubled.
