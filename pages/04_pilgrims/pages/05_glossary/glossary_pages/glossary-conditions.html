Conditions

Conditions are temporary effects which are applied to characters and modify their abilities.

Bleeding
When making Damage Rolls, a bleeding character rolls a minimum of 1d6, which cannot be taken by another player.

Dead
A dead character is sent to the Underworld.

Exhausted
Disadvantage on rolls for actions involving Stamina. This effect cannot be mitigated and is only removed with rest.

Fatigued
Disadvantage on rolls for actions involving Psyche. This effect cannot be mitigated and is only removed with rest.

Held
A held character cannot move and makes physical attacks with Disadvantage. A contested check is required to escape the hold.

Poisoned
Disadvantage on all checks.

Sealed
Character's maximum Psyche is halved until they are unsealed. Unsealing may be performed by a cleric with the proper knowledge.

Wounded
When a character is reduced to half of their health in one turn (note: current, not maximum), that player must roll 2d6 on the Wound Determination Table to see whether their character becomes wounded. If a character receives damage equal to more than half of their maximum health in one round, they do not roll on the Wound Determination Table and instead roll immediately on the Wounds Table.
