Weapons

Size: Small / Medium / Large
Size affects both the Stamina cost of weapon attacks and the damage dealt.

Range: Melee / Ranged
Range doesn't have any effect in combat, unless a character is outside the attack range of the enemy and is using a ranged weapon.

Complexity: Simple / Advanced
Advanced weapons may have additional effects and conditions, whereas Simple weapons will just do physical damage according to their size.

Quality: Regular / Reinforced / Broken
Reinforced weapons will Break instead of being Destroyed if a Break Roll result is 6, whereas Regular weapons will be destroyed.

Damage Type: Striking / Slashing / Piercing
Some enemies may be resistant, or even completely immune, to certain damage types. In these instances, the type of damage a weapon deals may be important. If players are able to come up with a reasonable way to get around these resistances, then the GM should allow them to do so. For example, if the party is facing an enemy that is resistant or immune to slashing and piercing damage, but is affected by blunt damage, a player using a sword may ask to use the hilt instead of the blade.

Wielding: One-Handed / Two-Handed
Many weapons may be wielded with two hands. Wielding weapons with two hands may increase damage dealt, but may inhibit other actions.

Size

Small

Small weapons cost 1 Stamina per attack and deal 1d6 physical damage based on their type.

Medium

Medium wepons cost 2 Stamina per attack and deal 2d6 physical damage based on their type.

Large

Large weapons cost 3 Stamina per attack and deal 3d6 physical damage based on their type.

Range

Melee

Melee weapons are limited to attacking targets in the immediate vicinity of the wielder.

Ranged

Ranged weapons are capable of hitting targets far from the wielder. Due to the way combat is handled, this will only affect attacks made out of combat.

Complexity

Simple

Simple weapons are just that: simple. They do not have any additional effects and are limited to dealing physical damage.

Advanced

Advanced weapons may have additional effects such as the ability to inflict conditions or deal non-physical damage, for example.

Quality

Regular

Regular is the most common quality for weapons, and offers no additional benefit.

Reinforced

Reinforced weapons have been altered by a blacksmith to be more resilient in combat. If a reinforced weapon would be destroyed, such as when making vigorous strikes, it becomes broken instead, and can be repaired by a blacksmith.

Broken

Weapons may become broken in myriad ways. Broken weapons may still be used in a pinch, but deal half damage and run the risk of being destroyed with each attack made. When attacking with a broken weapon, make a break roll for each attack made.

Destroyed

Weapons that have become destroyed are no longer usable, and cannot be repaired. They should be discarded from the players inventory immediately upon destruction.

Magical

Magical weapons can be used to make empowered strikes in combat without the need to make break rolls. However, if making vigorous strikes with Magical weapons, break rolls must still be made.

Damage Type

Striking

Striking damage is often most effective against armored enemies, and less effective against unarmored enemies.

Slashing

Slashing damage is often most effective against unarmored enemies, and less effective against armored enemies.

Piercing

Piercing damage offers a middle ground between Striking and Slashing, being generally effective against both armored and unarmored enemies.

Magical damage

Magical weapons offer an effective strategy for weapon wielding characetrs against enemies that are resistant to all types of physical damage.

Wielding

One-Handed

Wielding a weapon in one hand allows the character's second hand to be free for other uses, such as carrying, interacting with or using an item during combat.

Two-Handed

Wielding a weapon with both hands increases the damage dealt by 1d6, but requires the character to use both hands to hold their weapon. Wielding a weapon this way disallows the ability to interact with most items that turn.
