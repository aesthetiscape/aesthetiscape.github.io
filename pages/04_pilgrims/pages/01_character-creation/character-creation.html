Character Creation

Characteristics

Characteristics dictate general strengths and weaknesses when interacting with the world. The maximum score for each Characteristic is 20.

There are 3 Characteristics: Vitality, Stamina and Psyche. Each Characteristic is generated by rolling 3d6 and dropping the lowest die. If the result is 2, players may choose to re-roll the dice for that Characteristic.

Players may choose to roll the totals for all of their Characteristics and then assign them to create whatever character profile they desire, or may assign them in the order they are rolled and allow the dice to decide their specialty.

Vitality

A character's hardiness.

Vitality contributes directly to a character's pool of health points, as well as indirectly providing resistance to both physical and nonphysical attacks.

Stamina

A character's prowess.

All actions during combat, whether it be movement, weapon attacks, or casting spells, require Stamina.

Psyche

A character's fortitude.

All spells require Psyche to cast, but characters may also find other uses for Psyche, such as imbuing weapons strikes with magical effects, or using certain items.

Capabilities

Capabilities provide more fine tuning for their talents and skills. Each Capability is influenced directly by a corresponding Characteristic. The maximum score for Capabilities is 20.

Capabilities are generated by allocating the total value of a Characteristic between the two Capabilities connected to it, indicated by the first letter of the corresponding Characteristic.

Resilience

Represents resistance to physical damage. How well does the character withstand physical assault?

Will

Represents resistance to spiritual ailments. How well does the character withstand nonphysical effects?

Dexterity

Represents deftness. How able is the character to overcome physical obstacles through subtlety?

Strength

Represents power. How able is the character to overcome physical obstacles through brutality?

Insight

Represents belief. How understanding is the character of the workings of the spiritual world?

Intellect

Represents knowledge. How understanding is the character of the workings of the physical world?

Other Stats

Stats are values that exist for the benefit of the player to help them keep track of their character's well-being and progress.

Health Bonus

At character creation and at every level up, the player will roll 1d6, which is then added to their character's Health Bonus.

A character's Health Bonus is cumulative: Whenever Vitality or health bonus is increased, this is to help players easily recalculate their Health when it changes.

Health

Health is calculated by doubling the character's Vitality Characteristic and adding their Health Bonus:
(Vitality x 2 + Health Bonus)

Proficiency

Proficiency is a value which may be added to checks to accomplish actions. To begin with, a character's base proficiency score is 1, and players may choose two Unlinked Capabilities for their character to have proficiency in. For all checks made using a proficient Capability, the player will additionally add their Proficiency score to the result of the roll.

Experience

A measurement of what a character has gone through in their journey, experience is simplified to be a number of d6. When it is gained, characters should record the amount of experience received as Xd6.

Modifiers

Both Characteristics and Capabilities provide bonus modifiers when making checks and saves. Proficiency is another example of a modifier that may be added to rolls. Unless otherwise specified, multiple modifiers may be added to any roll.

At Start

All players begin their journey with a few essentials.

Skill Points

All characters start with four Skill Points to spend.

Click here for a list of all skills.

Starting Coin

A player's starting coin is determined by two rolls of 1d6. The first roll is multiplied by ten (i.e. a roll of 3 is 30 coins), and the second roll is multiplied by one (i.e. a roll of 3 is 3 coins).

Coin is the currency in traditional monetary transactions, however players may find that many merchants may be open to non-traditional payment.

Starting Gear

To begin, each character may choose two pieces of starting equipment.

Low Quality Armor

Armor protects against a certain amount of damage per round based on its weight, and can protect against a number of wounds based on its quality.

However, Medium and Heavy armors may hinder movement if strength is below a certain threshold.

Armor Weight and Location must be selected, but starting equipment armors are restricted to Low Quality.

Click here for an explanation of armors.

Simple Weapon

Weapons can be used to make physical attacks against enemies. Weapon damage is based on size, range and complexity.

Weapon Size and Range must be selected, but starting equipment weapons are restricted to Simple Complexity.
