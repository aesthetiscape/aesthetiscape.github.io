For the GM

Passage of Time

The following is a step-by-step flow to resolve one in-game day:

1. At the beginning of the day, unless they have a condition that inhibits rest or Stamina restoration, Stamina for all rested characters is restored to its maximum capacity.

2. All players who have rested recover Health equal to Xd6 + Vitality modifier, where X is equal to the character's Vitality modifier or 1, whichever is higher.

3. All players who have rested recover Psyche equal to Xd6 + Psyche modifier, where X is equal to the character's Psyche modifier or 1, whichever is higher.

4. The GM determines weather conditions for the day by rolling on the Weather Table.

5. The players decide on their course for the day.

6. The GM determines encounters, if applicable.

7. The GM describes the scenery and any points of interest that the party discovers, asking players for their actions, as required.

8. If there is an encounter, follow the steps outlined in Encounter below. Once the encounter is resolved, or if there is no encounter, progress to the next step.

9. The players have downtime, and then rest. At rest, each player rolls a d100, and if the result is at or below their current Curse total, they experience a Divine Encounter, outlined in the Divine Encounter section.

Encounter

If the GM is unsure regarding the non-player characters' reaction to the party, they may determine their reaction by rolling 1d6.

Roll Reaction
1 Hostile, cannot be reasoned with.
2-3 Hostile, open to reason.
4-5 Friendly, but suspicious.
6 Friendly, not suspicious.
Roll 1 2-3 4-5 6
Reaction Hostile, cannot be reasoned with. Hostile, open to reason. Friendly, but suspicious. Friendly, not suspicious.

If NPC reaction is known, one of the following happens:

1. Both sides wish to talk, the interaction is role-played.

2. The players wish to flee, the GM determines whether the non-player characters pursue.

3. Combat begins, follow the steps listed below.

Combat: Step-by-Step

Text about combat?

Determine Turn Order

1. Party rolls a number of d6 equal to their collective Dexterity Modifier, minimum of 1d6 per player.

2. Enemies roll a number of d6 equal to highest Soul Value.

3. Highest initiative takes the first turn.

One Round

Party Turn

1. All Party members note their character's current health. They decide on actions, and note Endurance and Psyche spent this round.

2. Party rolls for damage total, each player records Curse accrued.

Enemy Turn

1. GM notes Enemy Party health, as well as any Endurance and/or Psyche spent (if an ability is used).

2. GM rolls for enemy damage total.

3. Party members take turns, in order from lowest to highest Dexterity Score, making Damage Rolls until all damage is accounted for.

End of Round

1. Conditions Party members or Enemies may have received.

2. Any environmental effects.

3. Ensure Curse has been recorded.

4. Any special scenarios according to the GM's discretion.

5. All characters recover Stamina equal to 2d6 + Stamina Modifier unless a condition inhibits Stamina restoration.
